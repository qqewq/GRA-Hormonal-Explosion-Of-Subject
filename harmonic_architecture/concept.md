# Concept: Hormonal Explosion

## Philosophical background

The "hormonal explosion" is not a physical but an **aesthetic‑affective singularity**:
- The observer experiences a sudden, overwhelming wave of emotion – terror, ecstasy, tragic catharsis.
- This wave is *structured*: it follows a hidden logic, like a perfect horror movie climax.

In *What Ever Happened to Baby Jane?*, the final scene (Jane's breakdown on the beach) is a hormonal explosion:
- Local foam (Jane's madness, the sister's trauma) remains intact.
- Global foam (plot inconsistencies, irrelevant subplots) is nullified by the director's focus.
- The result is a single, unforgettable emotional peak.

## GRA translation

- **Local foam** – each element (film, theory, quote) keeps its internal contradictions and "personality".
- **Global foam** – conflicts *between* elements, semantic noise, structural redundancy.
- **Nullification** – iterative reduction of global foam while preserving local foam.
- **Hormonal index H** – a function of novelty, intensity, resolution, and surprise.

## The Baby Jane aesthetic in code

- Dark, noir‑like visualizations (foam as decaying film grain).
- Agents that can be "mad" – they refuse to give up their idiosyncrasies.
- A director‑agent that forces a coherent narrative arc upon the chaotic swarm.
